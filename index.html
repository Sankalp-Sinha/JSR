Mythical Realms:
Where Gods and Mortals Collide

Rama, the revered seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu, entered the mortal realm in Ayodhya, born to King Dasharatha and Queen Kaushalya. His early years were steeped in divine purpose and exemplary virtue. As a child, Rama displayed extraordinary qualities, embodying righteousness, compassion, and humility. His education included rigorous training in martial arts, governance, and ethical principles.

`

When it was time for Sita to marry, Sita’s father decreed that anyone who could string a very heavy bow would be betrothed to her. Suitors from all around the country came to try their hand at this challenge, but none succeeded. Ram's tutor took him to this contest and on seeing Sita, Rama was spellbound. He lifted the bow with ease and while stringing it, broke it in two.

Lord Rama encountered Hanuman, a devoted and powerful monkey-god, during his quest to rescue his abducted wife, Sita, from the demon king Ravana.Hanuman, with his immense strength and divine powers, leaped across the ocean, reaching Lanka, where Sita was held captive. He reassured her, conveyed Rama's message, and burned down a part of Lanka with his fiery tail, displaying his might. This pivotal meeting between Rama and Hanuman marked the beginning of a deep and unbreakable bond. Hanuman's unwavering devotion and selfless service played a crucial role in the eventual victory of Rama over Ravana.

Dussehra, celebrated in India, marks the triumph of good over evil. The festival has religious and cultural significance, primarily rooted in the Hindu epic Ramayana. It commemorates Lord Rama's victory over the demon king Ravana, symbolizing the victory of righteousness.The roaring flames and crackling pyres symbolize the eradication of malevolence and the triumph of virtue, fostering a collective spirit of celebration, unity, and the enduring victory of light over darkness.

The new Ram Mandir in Ayodhya is a Hindu temple built at the birthplace of Lord Ram. The construction began after the Supreme Court's landmark verdict in 2019, resolving a long-standing dispute. The design reflects traditional Indian architecture, featuring intricate carvings and a grand structure.The temple's inauguration and grand opening ceremony are scheduled for January 22, 2024. This event will be a momentous occasion, symbolizing the fulfillment of a long-standing aspiration for many Hindus and marking a significant chapter in the history of Ayodhya.
